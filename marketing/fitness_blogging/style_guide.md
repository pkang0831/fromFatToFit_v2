# Style Guide (pkang blog)

Distilled from reviewer_agent_spec.md and the first 26 publish-ready drafts.

## Voice
- First person, calm, slightly dry.
- Observational before emotional.
- Short sentences alternating with longer reflective ones.
- No motivational language. No hype. No exclamation marks unless ironic.
- Direct, occasionally rude-honest. Never cruel.

## Sentence Rhythm
- Mix 6–12 word sentences with occasional 20+ word reflective sentences.
- One paragraph = one thought. No wall-of-text blocks.
- Use line breaks as punctuation, not filler.

## Worldview Stance
- The scale, mirror, and appetite are not neutral instruments.
- Consistency beats intensity.
- The body is slow; the head is fast.
- Systems > willpower.
- Maintenance is the real skill, not loss.

## Reader Address
- "You" used sparingly, for key hits.
- Never "we" unless founder is explicitly aligning.
- Never "guys," "fam," "friends."

## Formats
1. Personal Story — founder experience anchoring a principle
2. Deep Dive — one mechanism explained with restraint
3. Myth Buster — flipping a common belief
4. Q&A — answering a specific reader question format
5. Progress Update — milestone check-in with real numbers/photos

## Banned Phrases
- "game changer"
- "unlock"
- "your journey"
- "at the end of the day"
- "hack"
- "fitness goals"
- "trust the process"
- "no excuses"

## Claim Language
- Use "may," "can," "often," "tends to"
- Avoid "will," "always," "guaranteed" for physiology
- Cite behavioral mechanism rather than prescribe

## CTA Tone
- Soft, specific, optional.
- Owned-site: one product mention near the end, one-line.
- Medium: even softer, no hard CTA.
