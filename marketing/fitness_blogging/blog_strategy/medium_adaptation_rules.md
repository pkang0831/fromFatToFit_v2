# Medium Adaptation Rules

This file defines how owned-site posts should be adapted for Medium.

Core principle:

- the owned site is the source of truth
- Medium is a distribution layer, not the home of the system

## What Stays The Same

- the title or a slightly improved title
- the emotional logic
- the core argument
- the founder voice
- the feeling of recognition and relief

## What Changes On Medium

### 1. Product CTA softens

Owned site:
- can use `Try the free body scan`
- can point directly to `/try`

Medium:
- should not read like a funnel page
- should not interrupt trust with heavy product framing

Preferred Medium close:

- `If this sounds familiar, the rest of my writing lives on Devenira.`
- `I write a lot about scale noise, body interpretation, and the ugly middle of dieting there.`
- `That is the world this piece comes from.`

### 2. Internal links become lighter

Owned site:
- full internal linking is good

Medium:
- keep links sparse
- use 1-2 relevant links max
- avoid overlinking every concept

### 3. Metadata becomes platform-native

For each Medium post define:
- Title
- Subtitle / deck
- 5 tags
- cover direction
- canonical intent if relevant

## Recommended Structure For Medium Versions

1. strong opening
2. body of article
3. no hard product pivot in the middle
4. calm closing
5. soft author / ecosystem note

## Author Close Template

Use one of these:

### Version A
`I write about weight loss, appetite, body image, and the slow work of learning how to read the body without panic.`

### Version B
`If this piece felt familiar, the rest of this writing lives inside the Devenira world.`

### Version C
`This is one piece of a larger body of writing about scale noise, visual proof, and the messy psychology of dieting.`

## Wave 01 Per-Post Adaptation Guidance

### 1. The Most Reliable Way to Succeed at Dieting Is Still the Least Dramatic One
- Medium goal:
  - worldview setter
- keep:
  - the “ugly little mood” opening
  - consistency identity close
- soften:
  - any explicit app framing
- best close:
  - worldview + writer identity

### 2. Why I Built Devenira for the Weeks Where You Want to Quit
- Medium goal:
  - founder trust
- keep:
  - personal story
  - 50kg context
  - invisible middle
- soften:
  - product CTA
- best close:
  - “this is why the writing exists”

### 3. Why the Mirror Can Make Real Progress Feel Fake
- Medium goal:
  - high saveability
- keep:
  - mirror as terrible historian
  - weekly visual record logic
- soften:
  - direct body-scan CTA
- best close:
  - better evidence > mirror panic

### 4. One Emotional Weigh-In Can Wreck a Good Week
- Medium goal:
  - high recognition
- keep:
  - scale story
  - interpretation framing
- soften:
  - direct conversion block
- best close:
  - “one loud morning should not run the whole process”

### 5. Read This Before You Try to “Fix” Your Diet Slip
- Medium goal:
  - comments and saves
- keep:
  - wrappers still there opening
  - pattern detection logic
- soften:
  - strong app bridge
- best close:
  - “you need fewer reasons to binge again”

### 6. Cheat Days Do Not Expose Your Character. They Expose Your System.
- Medium goal:
  - subscriber bait
- keep:
  - same pizza, different aftermath logic
  - cheat-day rebound framing
- soften:
  - product bridge
- best close:
  - “cheat days expose what the system still cannot absorb calmly”

## What Not To Do

- do not paste the owned-site CTA block unchanged
- do not make every Medium post end with the same branded sentence
- do not add too many product links
- do not make Medium feel like a lower-value duplicate

## Medium Wave 01 Deliverable Checklist

For each of the first 6 posts:

- final Medium title
- final subtitle
- 5 tags
- cover direction
- softened closing
- 1 optional link back to Devenira
