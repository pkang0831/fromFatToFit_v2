## Why these slot positions

Most Tier A slots land at position 2 of 5 (not first or last). Reasoning:

- Position 1 of a batch usually opens the most distinctive new editorial direction (founder/worldview tone). Tier A topics are deep_dive / myth_buster, not founder-led.
- Position 2 is the "sturdy mechanism" slot — a topic-focused deep_dive or myth_buster that grounds the batch before the more personal pieces in positions 3–5.
- Batch 14's Tier A goes at position 1 because sodium/water-weight is a strong batch-opener that paired well with the post-batch-13 voice momentum.

## Per-batch composition guidance (Tier A + 4 fresh)

Each batch keeps the same constraints:
- Format spread: max 2 same format consecutive (§12)
- Category spread: ≤3 per category in any 10-post window (§11)
- Tier A topic typically locks 1 category — write the 4 fresh slots to fill non-overlapping categories
- Voice: every Tier A draft uses extracted research notes (in `_archive_pre_blueprint/_research_notes_for_tier_a/`) as source material, NOT the old draft itself

## Workflow per Tier A draft

When writing the Tier A slot in its scheduled batch:

1. Read the research note file: `_archive_pre_blueprint/_research_notes_for_tier_a/<stem>_research_notes.md`
2. Optionally skim the old draft (`_archive_pre_blueprint/<stem>_publish_ready_OLD.md`) for mechanism details — DO NOT copy phrasing
3. Draft fresh under current `blog_post_blueprint.md` format
4. Apply `writer_pre_flight_class_checklist.md` (especially the Class 3 trigger checks specific to each Tier A's claim_class_floor)
5. Pick hero per current `image_pool_tightened_policy.md` rules
6. Internal links to existing posts.ts slugs
7. Reviewer subagent treats it identically to fresh drafts (no special handling)

## Why split across 5 batches (not all in one)

Three reasons:
- Topic spread per §11 — clustering 5 retro-topics into one batch would saturate certain lanes
- Voice continuity — fresh founder-anchored drafts in adjacent slots prevent the Tier A drafts (more mechanism-focused) from creating a voice dip
- Revisit risk — if the first Tier A rewrite produces a weak draft, the writer learns before committing to 4 more
