# Outreach Templates

Backlinks are the single biggest lever for moving the owned-site DR from
0 toward 30+. Until then, Medium is doing the heavy lifting. But every
backlink to either Medium or the owned site compounds.

This file contains templates you can copy-paste into outreach. Each one
is voice-matched (pkang's voice) and has been ruthlessly de-marketed.

---

## Reddit comment templates

### When someone asks about a topic you've written about

Voice rule: NEVER drop the link cold. Answer the question first in 4–8
sentences using the post's argument, THEN mention the link as
optional further reading.

```
> [quote of original question]

Yes — I went through this for a while. The pattern I noticed:

[2-3 sentence direct answer based on your post's thesis]

[1-2 sentences with a specific number / personal detail from the post]

If it helps, I wrote up the longer version of this with the math:
[your-medium-url + UTM tags]
```

### When someone shares a wrong or surface-level take

Voice rule: don't argue. Acknowledge the part that's true, then add
the missing piece. Cite YOUR experience, not "studies say."

```
A lot of that is right, especially the [specific thing they got right].

The piece I'd add is [what your post argues that they missed]. I went 
through the [specific phase] for [number] months and the thing that 
actually shifted was [non-obvious specific].

I wrote up that fuller pattern here if it's useful: [URL]
```

### Standalone link share (rare — only when post is THE answer)

Voice rule: use only when there's no better answer in the thread already.

```
I wrote about this exact thing recently — the part most plans miss is
that [post's load-bearing claim]. Full read here: [URL]

Happy to discuss in the thread.
```

## Hacker News submission templates

### Submission title

Use the post's SEO title (NOT the editorial title — HN audience prefers
direct intent). Examples:

| Post | HN-friendly title |
|------|-------------------|
| `wave_01_02` Why I Built Devenira... | "How I lost 50 kg over 5 years (and built a tool around it)" |
| `wave_03_07` Why Does Hunger Spike at Night... | "Why hunger spikes at night when dieting" |
| `wave_03_15` Daily Weighing... | "Daily weighing as a trap, not discipline" |

### First comment (post immediately after submission, ~1 paragraph)

Without this, the post has no chance — HN rewards thread starters who
add context.

```
Author here. Two notes that didn't fit in the post:

1. [a counterpoint or limitation of your own argument that you spotted
   after publishing]
2. [a specific data point or anecdote you almost cut]

Happy to answer questions in the thread.
```

### Best submit window

7:00–9:00 AM US Pacific on Tuesday/Wednesday/Thursday. Avoid weekends
(traffic but no front-page hold) and Mondays (firehose).

## Twitter / X reply templates

### When someone tweets about a topic you've covered

Voice rule: ANSWER first; LINK second (if at all). Don't be a link bot.

Pattern A (helpful reply, no link):

```
The part most diet content misses on this: [your post's central claim,
condensed to one sentence]. Took me [time period] to figure that out
the hard way.
```

Pattern B (helpful reply, link):

```
[Your central insight, 1-2 sentences].

Wrote it up if useful: [Medium URL with UTM]
```

### When someone tweets a question you've directly answered

```
Quick answer: [40-word direct answer].

Longer version with the math: [Medium URL with UTM]
```

### When sharing your OWN post

Voice rule: lead with the share-card line. Always include the cluster
context for context.

```
[Most quotable share-card sentence from the post]

[Cluster context — e.g., "More on the appetite-during-maintenance lane
in the latest at [URL]"]

[Medium URL]
```

## What NOT to do

These are common outreach failures. Avoid:

1. **Generic "you should check out my blog"** in any subreddit. Mods will
   ban you, and rightly. Read 5 threads before commenting.
2. **DMs to influencers asking for a "share"** — never works, burns
   the relationship.
3. **Buying backlinks / paying for guest posts**. Google penalizes the
   site, and the penalty doesn't come off cleanly.
4. **Mass-emailing every fitness newsletter editor**. Pick 5 you actually
   read; pitch each one a CUSTOM angle that proves you read them.
5. **Posting your own link to your own subreddit**. Always loses to
   organic traction; better to focus on writing the next post.

## Tracking outreach

Keep a simple Google Sheet (or markdown table here) with columns:

| Date | Channel | Type | Target | Post linked | Response | Outcome |
|------|---------|------|--------|-------------|----------|---------|

Outreach has a 3-5% response rate at best. You need volume + patience.
Track honestly so you can spot which channels return ROI.
