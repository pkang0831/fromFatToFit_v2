# Medium Launch Package

This folder is the practical launch kit for starting the blog on Medium before an owned site exists.

## Recommended Medium Position

Use a **personal Medium profile first**, not a full publication.

Why:

- faster setup
- less design overhead
- easier to start publishing immediately
- better for building a recognizable writer identity around `pkang`

Do **not** wait for perfect branding.

The real job of the first Medium phase is:

1. publish consistently
2. learn which hooks actually get read
3. learn which topics get saves, comments, and follows
4. build a visible archive under your own name

## What This Folder Contains

- `profile_and_positioning.md`
  Copy for your Medium bio, profile positioning, and how to describe the blog

- `first_6_launch_plan.md`
  The first six posts to publish, in order, with titles, subtitles, tags, and cover direction

- `publish_checklist.md`
  A Medium-specific checklist so you do not accidentally paste internal metadata or overproduce the posts

## Launch Principles

- lead with relatable pain, not with your résumé
- let readers discover your transformation through the writing and profile
- use Medium as a publishing base, not as a sales page
- avoid hard product promotion in the articles
- keep the visual system intentional: some real proof, some conceptual art, not random fitness glamour everywhere

Reason:

- strong emotional hooks
- high recognition value
- good mix of bingeing, hunger, snack cravings, cheat-day logic, and long-game consistency
- enough thematic overlap to feel like one world, not random blog spam

## Publishing Rhythm

Recommended starter rhythm:

- `3 posts per week`
- keep the posting days consistent
- publish the first 6 over `2 weeks`

Do not drop all 6 at once.

The goal is to give each article a chance to collect:

- reads
- highlights
- responses
- profile follows
