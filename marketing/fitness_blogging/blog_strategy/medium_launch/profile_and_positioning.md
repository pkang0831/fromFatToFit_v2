# Medium Profile And Positioning

## Core Positioning

You are **not** positioning as:

- generic fitness coach
- shredded motivation guy
- science-summary account

You are positioning as:

**a writer who lost 50 kg, became a professional model, and now writes about diet panic, appetite, body image, and how people keep misreading their own bodies**

That is stronger.
It is more human.
And it better matches the actual blog voice.

## Profile Name

Use:

`pkang`

Optional display variation:

`pkang | 50 kg lost`

Do not make the name sound brand-first.

## Short Bio Options

### Option 1

I lost 50 kg over five years and learned that most diet failure starts with bad body interpretation, not bad intentions. I write about appetite, weight loss, body image, and the psychology of not panicking.

### Option 2

Former panic dieter. Lost 50 kg. Ended up doing professional modelling. Now I write about hunger, body image, relapse, and why one bad weigh-in should not be allowed to ruin your life.

### Option 3

I went from 120 kg to 70 kg, then had to learn something harder than losing weight: how to stop misreading my body. Writing about dieting, appetite, body image, and staying sane through all of it.

## Longer About Paragraph

Use this for the profile intro or pinned “about me” story:

I lost 50 kg over roughly five years, going from 120 kg to 70 kg. What changed my life was not just losing fat. It was learning how often people quit because they misread normal body signals as proof of failure. I write about appetite, body image, diet relapse, food fear, and the slow work of learning how to read the body without panic.

## Tone Rules For Medium Profile

- calm
- credible
- slightly sharp
- not salesy
- not overly polished

Avoid:

- “helping thousands transform their lives”
- “science-based fitness educator”
- “DM me for coaching”
- app-first language
