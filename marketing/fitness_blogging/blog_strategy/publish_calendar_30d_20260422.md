## Per-day execution checklist

Use this 5-step sequence on each publish day:

1. **T-30 min**: Open the Medium package file. Verify SEO title,
   subtitle, meta description, tags, cover, and markdown body are
   the latest version (post-2026-04-22 SEO retrofit).
2. **T-0 (publish slot)**: Publish on Medium. Tags exactly as in package.
3. **T+5 min**: Copy Medium URL. Paste into:
   - `posts.ts` → matching post's `mediumUrl` field
   - `marketing/.../medium_launch/all_waves_tracker_20260422.md`
     manual fields row (date, URL)
4. **T+30 min**: Post X thread (replace `<MEDIUM_URL>` placeholder
   in the package). Pin the thread.
5. **T+2-6 hours**: Post LinkedIn copy (replace `<MEDIUM_URL>`).
   Publish at the next ET morning/lunch slot for max reach.
6. **T+48 hours**: Find one Reddit thread asking the package's
   primary keyword question (r/loseit, r/intermittentfasting,
   r/fitness30plus, r/xxfitness). Answer the question fully
   first, then close with `(I wrote about this if it helps: <link>)`.

## After 12 publishes — recalibration trigger

Once you've published 12 stories (mid-week 4 of this calendar):

1. Pull Medium account stats (views, reads, claps per story).
2. Re-run `python3 scripts/seo_retrofit/predict_engagement_ranges_20260422.py`
   and compare predicted vs actual.
3. Identify top-3 over-performers and bottom-3 under-performers.
4. Look at what the top-3 share (tag combo, opener style, length)
   and apply that pattern to the next 18 stories.
5. If a category systematically under-performs, deprioritize it
   for the second half of the calendar.

This gives you a 12-story-burn-in followed by a data-driven
pivot. Better than committing to all 30 stories blindly.
