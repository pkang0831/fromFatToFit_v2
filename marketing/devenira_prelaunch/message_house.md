# Message House

## Core Belief
Body change is hard to trust while it is still subtle.

## Big Promise
Devenira gives users a weekly proof loop so they can keep going with evidence instead of emotion alone.

## The Enemy
- mirror panic
- scale overreaction
- feature overload
- generic motivation
- apps that store data but do not reduce doubt

## Message Pillars

### 1. Weekly Proof Beats Daily Noise
What to say:
- The mirror is too slow.
- The scale is too noisy.
- Weekly check-ins show direction.

Proof points:
- weekly cadence is easier to sustain
- comparison matters more than one isolated reading
- progress photos plus AI interpretation create context

### 2. Built From a Real Transformation
What to say:
- this product exists because the founder lived the invisible middle
- the problem is emotional, not just technical
- the product is a response to doubt, not a random AI gimmick

Proof points:
- founder lost 50kg
- founder story explains why proof matters
- product language is grounded in lived experience

### 3. Privacy and Trust Are Part of the Product
What to say:
- body-photo products only work if trust is explicit
- explain storage, deletion, limits, and medical disclaimers clearly
- remove hidden assumptions

Proof points:
- photos are treated as sensitive
- trust section appears before final CTA
- no fake certainty about medical accuracy

## Message Ladder

### Top-level
`Track the change your mirror misses.`

### Mid-level
- `One scan is a number. Weekly check-ins are proof.`
- `Built for the week you want to quit.`
- `Proof over panic.`

### Support Claims
- start with one body check-in
- come back weekly to compare progress
- use AI to interpret change, not just store photos
- trust the trend before motivation catches up

## Channel Translation

### Landing Page
Focus on:
- what it is
- who it is for
- why weekly matters
- why users should trust it

### Instagram
Focus on:
- emotionally recognizable moments
- short statements that stop the scroll
- founder proof and anti-panic logic

### Blog / Medium
Focus on:
- the invisible middle of dieting
- why progress feels fake before it feels obvious
- why better evidence changes behavior

### Outreach
Focus on:
- `Does this problem feel familiar?`
- `Would you try this beta?`
- short, non-salesy conversation starters

## Voice Rules
- sound calm, not hype
- sound lived-in, not polished corporate
- use short sentences when the emotional point matters
- explain one idea at a time
- make the reader feel understood before offering the product

## Words To Repeat
- proof
- weekly
- trend
- mirror
- panic
- visible change
- baseline
- check-in

## Words To Reduce
- ecosystem
- optimization
- revolutionary
- biohacking
- ultimate
- transformation engine

## CEO Sanity Check
Before publishing anything, ask:

1. Does this make the product sound simpler?
2. Does this make the user feel understood?
3. Does this lead back to weekly proof?
4. Does this sound like a real person wrote it?
