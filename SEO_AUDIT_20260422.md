# SEO Audit — Phase A (2026-04-22)

Scope: production site `www.devenira.com`, 69 blog posts after the
2026-04-22 rollout (v2/main @ `3b51603`).

Read-only audit across 10 dimensions. Zero files modified in this
phase. Output is a ranked action list for Phase B/C.

---

## Executive summary

**Infrastructure status**: ✅ Good. SEO fields are 100% populated
(seoTitle, metaDescription, keywords, cluster, schemaType,
lastModified). JSON-LD is complete and valid. webp heroes everywhere.
Redirects wired. Sitemap complete.

**Content quality status**: ❌ Poor. The fields are filled, but the
substance is weak. The three highest-leverage gaps, in priority order:

1. **Primary keyword is NOT in the first 100 words** on 49 of 69 posts
   (71%). Google weights intro paragraph heavily for relevance scoring.
2. **Content is thin**: 59 of 69 posts (86%) are under 1,500 words.
   p50 = 888 words. Competitors rank with 2,000–3,000 word in-depth posts.
3. **E-E-A-T presentation is invisible to humans**: author byline,
   author photo, author bio, and external citations are all missing
   from the rendered page. Only metadata has them.

These three are more impactful than anything we did in the rollout.

### A3 — Primary keyword placement ⚠️ **HIGH PRIORITY**

| Check | Count | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| primary kw in seoTitle | 50 / 69 (72%) | ⚠️ 19 missing |
| primary kw in metaDescription | 59 / 69 (86%) | ⚠️ 10 missing |
| primary kw in first 100 words of body | **20 / 69 (29%)** | ❌ 49 missing |
| any keyword in first 100 words | **20 / 69 (29%)** | ❌ 49 missing |

**The 49-missing gap in the intro is the single biggest SEO finding
in this audit.** Google's algorithm weights the opening paragraph
heavily for topic relevance. Posts that don't mention the target
keyword in the first ~100 words are effectively invisible for that
query regardless of how good the title tag is.

Representative examples (flagged slug → primary kw not found in intro):
- `how-i-lost-50-kg-middle-of-diet` → "how i lost 50 kg" (!!)
- `how-to-break-a-weight-loss-plateau` → "how to break a weight loss plateau"
- `how-to-handle-hunger-pangs-on-a-diet` → "how to handle hunger pangs"
- `why-am-i-so-hungry-after-lifting-weights` → "why am i hungry after lifting"

### A5 — Heading hierarchy

Each post renders H2 per "titled" section. Avg H2 per post: 4–6
(matches industry norm for 1,500-word articles).

**No posts violate hierarchy** — H1 is present once (the title), H2s
via titled sections, H3s for FAQ summaries inside the FAQ accordion.

### A6 — Internal link density

The blog body content is plain-text (no inline hrefs), so **post-to-
post internal linking inside article body = 0**. Internal linking
only happens via:
- Topic cluster pillar page (`/blog/topic/<cluster>`)
- "More articles" cards at end of each post (auto-generated by
  `getRelatedBlogPosts`, 2 cards per post)
- `/blog` index

Verdict: ⚠️ Minimal. Competitors typically have 3–5 inline contextual
links per post. Missing this signal loses semantic-relevance ranking.

Opportunity: For each post, add 2–3 inline `[anchor text](/blog/slug)`
cross-references to related posts. Requires content-level edits or a
post-build linkifier.

### A10 — E-E-A-T signals ⚠️ **HIGH PRIORITY**

This is the biggest hidden risk. Google's YMYL (Your Money or Your
Life) guidelines explicitly require visible expertise signals on
health/weight-loss content. Our **metadata is complete** but our
**human-visible presentation is sparse**.

#### What metadata has (Google sees)

- `<link rel="author" href="https://devenira.com/authors/pkang">` ✅
- `<meta name="author" content="pkang">` ✅
- `<meta property="article:author" content="pkang">` ✅
- JSON-LD `Article.author` with full Person object (name, url, image,
  description, knowsAbout[]) ✅
- JSON-LD Person at /authors/pkang ✅

#### What the rendered page has (human sees)

| Element | Status |
|---|---|
| "By pkang" visible byline at top | ❌ **not rendered** |
| Author photo visible | ❌ **not rendered** |
| Author bio footer ("About pkang" block at end) | ❌ **not rendered** |
| Clickable author link from post page | ❌ **not rendered** |
| "Published" + "Updated" dual-date display | ⚠️ only single date shown |
| External authoritative citations in body | ❌ none |
| Reading time | ✅ shown |
| Tags | ✅ shown |

The `BlogArticleContent.tsx` renders: topic/date/reading-time row →
tags → H1 → deck → hero → body → CTA → related. **No author box
anywhere visible to humans**.

Impact: On health queries Google wants to see who the author is and
why they're credible, visibly. Invisible metadata helps machine
parsing but not the ranking signals tied to user trust behaviors
(dwell time, return visits).

#### External citations

Body content is purely personal experience (N-of-1). For science-
leaning queries (appetite, hormones, metabolism, leptin/ghrelin),
Google ranks posts with links to research higher. Our posts don't
cite a single study.

## Recommended Phase B order

Do TOP 5 first. They compound:

**Phase B1 (est. 1h)** — Add visible author box + byline to
`BlogArticleContent.tsx`. Single component edit, propagates to all 69
posts. Biggest E-E-A-T win per hour spent.

**Phase B2 (est. 3–4h)** — Script-assisted first-100-words keyword
injection. For each of the 49 flagged posts, rewrite the first
paragraph to include the primary keyword naturally while preserving
voice. Use the existing `apply_*.py` script pattern.

**Phase B3 (est. 2–3h)** — Inline internal linking. Build a linkifier
that maps post slugs to keywords and auto-injects 2–3 hrefs per post
(with review).

**Phase B4 (est. 4–6h)** — Expand the 8 sub-500-word posts. Each post
gets 3–5 new H2 sections with substantive content. Individual
judgment needed; not fully scriptable.

**Phase B5 (est. 3h)** — External citation pass on the 20 science-
leaning posts (appetite/hormones/metabolism). Add 1–2 Pubmed/MASS/
Examine.com links per post as authority signals.
