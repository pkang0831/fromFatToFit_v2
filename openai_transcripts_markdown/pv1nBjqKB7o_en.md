The fat in the human body is divided into visceral fat and subcutaneous fat.

Visceral fat is the fat that is located next to the organs and is situated deeper than the abdominal muscles.

Just like we can't touch our internal organs by feeling our bodies, it's fat that cannot be touched.

On the other hand, subcutaneous fat is located just beneath the skin and is fat that is outside of the muscles.

It's the fat that you can feel when you grab the skin with your hand.

In terms of health, visceral fat, which directly affects the organs, is more of a problem.

In beauty, subcutaneous fat is a bigger issue.

However, these two types of fat have different functions in the body.

There are differences in the methods of removal.

Since visceral fat is located right next to the organs, it has the characteristic of being easily utilized as energy.

So when you adjust your diet, it effectively reduces fat.

However, since subcutaneous fat is located on the outermost layer of the body, excluding the skin,

It is fat that is difficult to utilize for energy.

It is fat that protects the body from external impacts rather than being used for energy, or helps to maintain body temperature.

So, it's the fat that effectively reduces when you exercise rather than focusing on diet.
